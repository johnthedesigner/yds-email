Hello dahlia friends!

We imagine over the past few weeks, like us you have been busy planting your tubers. The heat wave was brutal but a little determination, sweat and lots of water. It’s rewarding when the sprouts start to emerge from the soil and the weeks seem to fly by, especially when you are having fun! Mid May we did some bed preparation, weeding and added compost to the dahlia beds at Maplebrook Farmstead. Thank you to Victoria Mei for helping with preparing the beds for planting. Late in May and early June, Carol and I had the pleasure to spend a few days days helping to plant club tubers at three of our growing partners: Sharon Hoskins from Brilliant Dahlia Flower Farm, Emily Round from Grounded Flower Farm and Nancy Miller from Maplebrook Farmstead. We were working hard planting just shy of 800 tubers in our first three weeks. It took a ton of tuber sorting and organization. Thank you to Andrea Campbell for pitching in for a few hours at the studio to help us out. We still have a little more work ahead of us and are scheduled to plant another 400 tubers collectively over the next week at Woburn Senior Center, Wright-Locke Farm and Petersfield Farm.

Would you like to help us plant tubers? We would love to do it together, whatever time you can give. Email info@yankeedahliasociety.com Subject: Count Me In For Planting!

We are truly grateful and proud of having so many unique partnerships in our first year. Sharing the joy of growing dahlias while supporting local small farm business and community outreach programs is so rewarding. We look forward to introducing each one of them to you throughout the year.

Last weekend we also took a beautiful ride to Farmington, New Hampshire to meet members Melissa & Chris from Shepherd's Gate Flower Farm. We were taken on a wonderful tour of their gardens enjoying the spring blooms and numerous dahlia beds sprouting with beautiful leafy plants. It was a pleasure to meet such a talented couple with both horticulture and floral design gifts.

In addition to planting club tubers and our own gardens we have been busy working on the schedule for upcoming meetings. We are very excited about getting together for club meetings as the blooming season will be upon us in just a few short months. You’ll want to save the date for our rescheduled May meeting that will be held on Sunday, July 18th. Rain or Shine we will have a tent that will keep us either shaded or dry during the day. Also see the schedule below as there are a few more club dates to save on your calendar and not to be missed. We are planning a potluck club BBQ in August. We are very excited to have a garden tour on September 19th at Y.D.S. member Robin Sears’ home in South Hamilton, MA. We have already made arrangements to be back at Maplebrook Farmstead for our October meeting.

– Misty & Carol

Dahlia Tip

Filling holes in your dahlia garden

Did you plant a tuber and still don’t see signs of life? It’s been over 5 weeks, nothing has sprouted and all you want to do is scream? Have you taken the step to dig around where you know you had planted a tuber? You discover that the tuber is completely mush and rotted, or that there must not have been an eye and therefore no sprout. Well don’t worry, we have grown fully hardened off rooted dahlia cuttings that you can purchase from Y.D.S. to fill that hole instantly. If you have a bare spot you are looking to fill or a friend that decided they want to try to grow dahlias too, or you just can’t resist the urge to squeeze a few more dahlias into your garden, we have you covered! See our PDF list. We will coordinate delivery/pick-up. Limited quantities of many choice varieties; act now and email info@yankeedahliasociety.com Subject: I need some more Dahlias

July Meeting

Our First In-Person Meeting Club Meeting!

Join Y.D.S. at Maplebrook Farmstead, one of our growing partners

Our very first in-person meeting is rescheduled for Sunday, July 18th at Maplebrook Farmstead in Sterling, MA. Rain or Shine. We will have a large tent to gather under for shade or in case of rainy weather.

It should be a nice summer afternoon for our first ever in-person club meeting! Bring your dahlia enthusiasm and join us for some fun at the farm. Joel Betts, Conservation Planner with Worcester County Conservation District, will be with us to demonstrate soil sampling and answer all your soil questions. Vijaya (Vee) Maclean from Best Buds Worm Castings will talk about soil nutrition and biology, and will have her soil amendment products available for purchase. We will give demonstrations of planting tubers, staking plants, taking cuttings, dividing, pinching, and sharing tips to make your growing season a success. Bring chairs and a picnic lunch, and see the farm as we kick off the growing season and meet our growing partners and members. We will come back in October to see what the farm looks like in full bloom.

Maplebrook Farmstead farm stand will be open and have goodies and aged manure for purchase. For aged manure, $5/Bucket, BYO bucket & fill your own bucket.
