Hello dahlia friends!

Our very first in person club meeting is in the books! It was wonderful to see so many of you at Maplebrook Farmstead in July. Thank you again to our wonderful presenters Joel Betts of Worcester County Conservation District and Vee Maclean from Best Buds Worm Castings for sharing their time and expertise. In the meantime, after such a rainy summer it’s so exciting to see the posts on Facebook/Instagram as everyone's blooms are starting to explode.

– Misty & Carol

Y.D.S. Growing Partners Introduction

Grounded Flowers (Winchester, MA)

By Misty Florez

Wright-Locke Farm is a community farm located in Winchester, Massachusetts with a rich history dating back to the 17th century. The town of Winchester purchased the 13 acres in 2007 and a Master Planning Committee and ultimately the Wright-Locke Farm Conservancy were created. The charm, history and the architectural significance of one of the few intact early nineteenth century farmsteads are represented in the six structures including the 1828 original Farmhouse, the 1827 Barn, the Squash House and the Ice house. The newest addition, the All-Seasons Barn, was recently completed and will offer year round space for events and educational activities. The Farm is a special location creating a strong community through the variety of CSA programs, seasonal U-pick flowers and raspberries, educational classes and workshops for all ages. We are thrilled to be able to assist with growing and maintaining dahlias while offering educational workshops and connecting with the community through the Farm.

The Farm is only a few miles away from the home of Misty Florez, club Co-President, making this partnership with a satellite dahlia garden for Y.D.S. a wonderful opportunity. The dahlia garden is located in a sunny spot next to the Farm’s beautiful cut flower field. As the Farm had little previous experience in growing dahlias, Y.D.S. is thrilled to share our knowledge and expertise with them in growing and enjoying dahlias. The cut dahlias will be used in flower arrangements sold at the farm and the tuber clumps will be harvested for future Y.D.S club stock and sales.

We are maximizing the number of dahlias in the dedicated area, however it was recognized early on in the season that the location was in need of some soil improvement. Y.D.S. donated the resources and materials needed to revitalize the area for the dahlia garden. A few lumber boards, organic loam-cow compost sourced from Catlin Farmstead (otherwise known as Otter River Black Gold), some elbow grease and in just a few days six new raised beds were built! Later this month the addition of a new entry gate and simple fence to keep the bunnies and chickens at bay will put the finishing touches on the project. We can’t wait to see the garden in bloom!
