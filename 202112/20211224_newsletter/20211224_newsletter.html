Happy Holidays!

The end of the year always seems to go by so fast around the holidays. Our dividing weekends over the past two months have been such a success and we are truly grateful to all of the members that have been able to help us divide and those that have mailed in tubers to donate to the club sale and stock. It has been a blast to get to know so many of you while we divide tubers. A handful of our person meetings will be held at Spence Farm in Woburn, MA. It is a wonderful space owned by the town and run by the Woburn Recreation Department.

We will celebrate our 1st year as a club and the new growing season with a New Year Party! It will be a ton of fun, you won’t want to miss this. Be sure to save the date. Fun dahlia themed raffle items to choose from as well as door prizes. For those that purchase from the Y.D.S tuber sale in January, this will be your opportunity to come pick up your dahlia tuber order.

In the meantime we are working really hard to get as many of our tubers divided prior to our sale. For those that are available and would like to help we are trying to do a push for the next two weeks. We’ve added dividing dates to our SignUp Genius. If you have a few hours to help out check out the link.

Member Spotlight

Sharon Hoskins from Brilliant Dahlia Flower Farm

Photography by: Sharon Hoskins

Website: brilliantdahlia.com

Instagram: @brilliantdahliaflower

Sharon Hoskins has been passionate about growing flowers since she and her husband, Erik bought an old chicken farm in 1998. Each year, the family's garden beds multiplied and so did their family. With three growing and active boys, two careers and a lot of pets, farming their land was a dream that held a special place in their hearts for many years before they could do anything serious about pursuing their dream.

In December 2019, Sharon retired from a 24 year career in federal law enforcement and finally had time to put the large acreage farm to work. She enrolled in the Floret's Flowers course in January 2020 and started Brilliant Dahlia Flower Farm, ("BDFF") shortly after. Never one to be shy, Sharon offered flower CSAs that first year in business before she even had flowers planted in the ground (besides her perennial beds.) Sharon's original plot was designed to be a 50' x 50' annual bed which soon became a 83' x 99' plot after her also not shy husband, started prepping the field. Clearly, husband and wife were more than excited to start this new venture.

The farm specializes in dahlias and grew over 200 varieties in 2021. They also have a large collection of peonies, hypericum, David Austin Roses, lilacs, fragrant narcissus and many perennials. Annual staples of their farm include strawflowers, tulips, sunflowers, lisianthus, ranunculus and much much more.

BDFF is now entering their third year in business. In the first two years, flower CSAs have been a strong base of their business model. In year two, the farm was also able to host some specialty classes and workshops on their farm to support their mission to include the local community in sustainable agriculture. Coming into year three, the farm is excited about the prospects of having more events on the farm, including flower to farm dinners and other speciality events.

BDFF is situated in Rehoboth MA, a Right to Farm community, where farms are becoming more rare. In the past five years, local developers have taken over some of the larger parcels of farms and golf courses that used to fill the landscape, so preserving BDFF as a flower farm is even more important to Sharon & Erik now, than it was when they bought that old chicken farm in 1998.

Dahlia Tip

Super Glue Tuber Necks

If a tuber has a viable eye and an unfortunately weak neck (or even slightly damaged), it may just be the right candidate to save from tossing into the compost bin. All you need is a little super glue. It’s worth a try, right?!

Not every tuber can be saved or is worth the effort of gluing, but just sometimes you can see that just a few of the fibers around the neck have broken and a bit of glue might just be the trick.
