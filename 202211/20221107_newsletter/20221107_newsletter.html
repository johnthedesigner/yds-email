Hello dahlia friends!

September and October have been a whirlwind of activity! Working in the gardens, attending local shows, giving a presentation to a local garden club, holding a member garden tour, traveling to the ADS National Show and preparing the details for our first YDS Dahlia Bloom Exhibit all filled the month of September. Then we jumped right into the first weekend in October holding our successful YDS Dahlia Bloom Exhibit. Floral design artist TJ McGrath was our guest via Zoom for our October meeting, demonstrating how to create a beautiful floral design using only dahlias. Following a busy start to the month, we managed to find time to take images of dahlias for our club tuber sale while making sure bloom labels were moved and plants verified, scheduling the end of the season at our growing partner locations and cutting back, lifting, washing and preparing all club tubers to be ready to divide, and putting calls out for volunteer assistance, all of which has kept us very busy.

New this year, YDS will be holding a Dahlia-O-Rama at Spence Farm in Woburn. We are setting aside Sunday, December 4th from 7:00am to 7:00 pm as a one day club event to get our club tubers divided and ready for our tuber sale, and we would love to get all hands on deck to join in and help us out. Whether you are an experienced divider, or new to the process, there will be something for everyone to do. Just like last year, members who volunteer at least six hours of time will get Early Access for a full week to our online tuber sale before we open it up to all our members. December 4th is a great opportunity to get your volunteer hours in for Early Access if you can’t come at any other time between now and then. Be sure to check out the details and SignUp Genius to participate.

Looking forward to seeing you online for our November meeting via Zoom next week!

– Misty & Carol

Our September Garden Tour & Meeting

YDS Member Garden Tour with YDS Members Susan and Brad Keyes

Photos by John Livornese & Misty Florez

Our September meeting was a wonderful tour of the garden of YDS members Susan and Brad Keyes. So many of our members and guests enjoyed a really beautiful day in Susan and Brad's garden, filled with perennials and lots of dahlias in full bloom. The morning photography session included a presentation given by John Livornese and Misty Florez on some of the tips and techniques we can try to help us take more beautiful photos of our flowers. technical aspects of composing a photograph. John and Misty shared many excellent pointers to add interest to your photos such as: look for movement, and take advantage of backlighting. The afternoon presentation given by Carol and Misty was the beginning in the series of our Dahlia Study program. It was a deep dive into understanding dahlia forms through petal structures.

The day ended with a tour of the dahlia garden led by Susan. We thank Susan and Brad for opening their garden to YDS, for their generous hospitality (and Brad's delicious homemade peach tarts!) and for the wonderful time had by all who attended.

Our October Zoom Meeting

Special Guest Floral Design Presentation

TJ McGrath of TJ McGrath Design

In October we enjoyed a wonderful Zoom meeting with a demonstration by floral design artist TJ McGrath https://www.tjmcgrathdesign.com . In an engaging presentation TJ showed us how he creates a beautiful floral design using dahlias alone. TJ invited us all to freely ask questions, and took time to thoughtfully answer every one. He explained many of the design tips he considers as he creates a floral arrangement, such as creating the design by working from the outside in; and demonstrated how he can so successfully incorporate blooms with unusual stem shape to add movement and visual interest to each arrangement.

TJ created his floral design in a beautiful pottery bowl made especially for him for his demonstration by YDS member and ceramicist Justin Reis https://www.justinreisceramics.com/ . Our thanks to both of them for their successful collaboration for our October meeting.

Member Spotlight

Berns Nera

bmcnera

Photos by: Berns Nera

My first foray into growing dahlias began 4 years ago when I bought several tubers at the Tower Hill Botanic Garden plant sale. While I've been growing veggies such as tomatoes and zucchini for years, it never crossed my mind to grow flowers as well. Unfortunately, my first year growing dahlias was a failure. That summer we were moving and I didn't have a chance to plant the dahlia tubers in the ground until mid June. Since we had a lot of rain that June, my tubers rotted and nothing sprouted my first year.

As a scientist by training, I was determined to grow dahlias and spent some time researching how to grow them. The following spring, I planted my tubers in pots to get a head start and ensure a seedling emerged. Once the weather was warm enough, I transplanted the dahlias in the ground and waited for the flowers to bloom.

Since 2018, I've been growing dahlias every year and have started to grow other flowers such as zinnias, bearded irises, and roses. I love planting flowers alongside the veggies and seeing all the pollinators in the garden. One of my favorite things about gardening is sharing the harvest of veggies and flowers.

This year, I am currently growing 40 dahlia plants. While I am a fan of the ball and formal decorative varieties, I'm growing my first single form dahlia: 20th Ave Sunset. Towards the end of the season I am planning on saving some dahlia seeds. As I love having fresh flowers around the house, I would also love to learn how to arrange flowers. I always feel like I don't grow enough fillers and would love to know your favorites.

While I don't post often on instagram, I'd love to connect - @bmcnera

ADS National Dahlia Show: September 22 - 25, 2022

By Carol Palmer and Misty Florez

Photos by Misty Florez

The 2022 ADS National Show was hosted by National Capital Dahlia Society in Reston, Virginia at the end of September. Carol and Misty took a six day trip and drove down to Virginia on a Wednesday and enjoyed the week filled with dahlia activities.

It was Carol’s first time attending a National ADS show and we were very excited to be able to bring blooms from our garden and enter them in the show. It was a spur of the moment decision, and it was a great learning experience. No head table blooms for us but we are proud to have received a few ribbons in our sections. At the show Misty was able to participate in judging blooms while Carol was a clerk on a judging team. It’s a priceless experience to attend and participate in a National show, meet lots of wonderful people, work on a team with experienced dahlia growers, judges and mingle with hybridizers and growers from around the country and around the world.

While in Virginia we also had a chance to visit YDS Member Lucinda Eye who lives in Virginia. Lucinda joins us for our club Zoom meetings and we were excited that it would only be a short drive to get to meet her in person. We were in Mennonite country and saw horse drawn buggies and had a delicious lunch. The visit of course included lots of conversation about dahlias, meeting with her sister and friends and a visit around Lucinda’s garden.

While touring Lucinda’s garden we knew her dahlia blooms were show quality and encouraged her to have us bring her blooms to the National Show and stage them for her. We are excited for her blue ribbons!

All the way from Australia we also had the chance to hang out with our friend THE Warren Vigor. Just as much the wonderful person we know via Instagram we love in person too. Congratulations Warren on your ADS Silver Medal! The Dahlia Community has truly enjoyed your visit to the United States.

Mid Atlantic Trial Garden Visit (Deerwood, MD)

While in Virginia we were taken on a tour by the National Capital Dahlia Society to visit the Mid-Atlantic Trial Garden. It is one of the seven American Dahlia Society Trial Gardens across the country where hybridizers send in new seedling entries to be scored by ADS Judges. It was a wonderful opportunity to learn what dahlia judges evaluate in a new seedling. Thank you to NCDS for being such wonderful hosts as we had a wonderful trip!

Join or Renew Your ADS Membership

The American Dahlia Society (ADS) is organized to promote and encourage the development of and appreciation of the dahlia through educational and research activities and by supporting the more than 60 local dahlia societies who are a part of ADS.

Whether you are interested in growing seedlings or hybridizing, showing dahlias, growing dahlias as cut flowers, or just want to know more about them, you should definitely consider joining ADS. ADS publishes a very informative and very attractive full color Bulletin three times a year along with an annual Classification and Handbook of Dahlias that is an invaluable reference. YDS Dahlia Bloom Exhibit

By Misty Florez & Carol Palmer

Photos by Misty Florez, John Livornese & Andrea Campbell

Photo by: TBD

On October 1-2 YDS held our first ever public Dahlia Bloom Exhibit at the Westwood Library. Collectively many of our YDS members and local dahlia growing friends brought in hundreds of beautiful blooms and several arrangements to fill the room for the public to enjoy. Blooms were arranged according to form across several tables and labeled with the variety name where it was known. Along with all the many individual blooms there was an entire table of lovely dahlia arrangements.

In addition, each afternoon YDS held a free presentation for the public to attend. We’d like to thank YDS member Chau Ho who gave a wonderful presentation on Beautiful Bouquets with Dahlias from Your Garden. Chau showed us how easy it is to create beautiful arrangements with the flowers we already have in our gardens. Misty and Carol gave a presentation the following day on The Joy of Growing Dahlias, sharing with attendees some of the history and biology of dahlias along with the beauty dahlias can bring to their gardens.

Inspired by the floral foam free dahlia shows in California we decided not to use floral foam in our display to hold up blooms. In considering the logistics of dahlia show displays, we reached out to Heather Jamieson of Central Coast Dahlia Society in California who provided us with invaluable assistance and information. We are grateful to Heather for her help. Brainstorming logistics for some of the larger and taller blooms with help from Andrea Campbell we created one hundred chicken wire balls in two different sizes and also used floral pin frogs to hold up the blooms. We are excited that these were for the most part successful in our first attempt at not using floral foam and having a wonderful display; and we look forward to refining the support and display for bigger blooms. The clean up was so much easier than having hundreds of pounds of wet floral foam to dispose of. We are looking forward to refining the ways to steady and display the larger blooms and appreciate the positive support we received as we try something a little different.

Thank you to all of you who brought blooms and arrangements, helped out with staging and breakdown, to Chau for presenting and Andrea for creating chicken wire balls, to members for helping out one another to stage blooms and for those that came out to view and supported the YDS Dahlia Bloom Exhibit. It was well attended and you all made it a fantastic event.

Brilliant Dahlia Flower Farm: Growing Dahlias in a Greenhouse & Field Grown Dahlias

Article & Photos By: Sharon Hoskins

Edited by: Carol Palmer

Growth

To truly enjoy the beauty of my dahlias year-round, I’ve developed a secondary passion...that of photography to capture my dahlias in full bloom. While some may feel an expensive digital camera is needed, I use nothing more complex than my own iPhone. As it took a lot of trial and error to start getting consistently solid results, here are my tips for digitally preserving your dahlias forever:

Challenges

Thankfully, there are no more signs of slimy mold in the tunnel which I would remove with plastic bags at the first sign. However, now I am battling powdery mildew on the greenery. To combat this, I have removed lower leaves from the infected plants and run a fan on the two rows I have to simulate a breeze on a daily basis.

Temperature

My tunnel has doors on each end that we have kept open since before summer. We also have both of the sides rolled up on the east and west sides and the roof vented when it’s not raining, which has basically been the entire summer until most recently. While some air gets in the tunnel with all the vents, my thoughts are that there hasn’t been enough air flow with the high heat we had at the start of the year and that contributed to the powdery mildew. The shade cloth is up and has brought relief to all the plants in the tunnel. The shade cloth will definitely go up sooner next year.

Concluding Notes

I have resolved not to grow dahlias in a high tunnel again. While I am very grateful to have the tunnel, I rushed to try as many different cut flowers to grow in it as I could. Looking back, that probably wasn’t the best decision. So my plan for next year is to grow tulips and ranunculus in the tunnels. Once those are done, I plan to rotate in heat loving summer crops such as different types of celosia which I love to use for dried flowers, and sunflowers. My dahlias will stay outside next year for sure. Now my biggest job is to ensure I save the inside tubers from any disease to give these tubers a shot at thriving outside next year. They will also be marked to be planted away from my other tuber stock so I can watch them for signs of repeated disease next year.

Dahlias In Australia “The Winkie Way” by John Menzel

by: Carol Palmer

Photo by: Misty Florez

Photos by Wendy Bedrosian

YDS is really excited to announce that we now have copies of John Menzel’s excellent book Dahlias In Australia “The Winkie Way” available for purchase! John Menzel (1948-2021) was an Australian dahlia grower and hybridizer known for his beautiful “Winkie” introductions. Over his long career of growing, showing and hybridizing dahlias, John gained a wealth of experience which he shares in this book. Dahlias In Australia is considered by many to be the definitive work on growing dahlias, and we share that opinion. Loaded with full color photographs and with clear and concise text, Menzel’s book covers every aspect of dahlias from growing and propagating, showing, hybridizing and much, much more. This book is a must-have resource for your own dahlia library and a wonderful gift for every dahlia lover on your gift list! 126 pages. Price $45.
