Are you ready for Sunday's meeting?

The Science of Flower Color and Searching for the Blue Dahlia

Sunday, June 18, 2023 from 11:00am to 1:30pm

*arrive 15 minutes before to get settled and say hello.

Presentation by Dr. Virginia Walbot, Stanford University Professor of Biology, Emerita

Left: Photo by Misty Florez
Right: Dr. Virginia Walbot, Stanford University

Modern dahlias come in an astounding variety of colors, from crimson to yellow, purple to white; with an almost infinite number of shades in between, from the most saturated hues to the softest pastels. Add in the many possible bicolor combinations that may show up in a dahlia bloom, and you have a riotous color extravaganza for your garden. Have you ever wondered why flowers have color in the first place, and why modern dahlias have such a wide range of colors? What exactly is it that makes a dahlia purple, or pink, or yellow; and how does a dahlia plant produce its color?

Our presenter Dr. Virginia Walbot, retired Professor of Biology at Stanford University has spent a number of years doing research on genetics in corn and dahlias and is a renowned expert in her field. With Kristine Albrecht, she began the Genome Project for the American Dahlia Society in 2016, seeing it through Phase One. Dr. Walbot has most recently been selected as the 2023 recipient of The Barbara McClintock Prize for Plant Genetics and Genome Studies by the Maize Genetics Cooperation for her lifetime contributions to the fields of plant genetics and genomics.

Join us as Dr. Virginia Walbot shares a slide presentation to explain the intriguing science of color in flowers. We will learn what anthocyanin is and what role it plays in dahlia color, whether color in dahlias is determined by environment or genetics, and why the color blue remains such an elusive goal in hybridizing dahlias. Bring your questions, there is so much to learn! Whether you are actively hybridizing or selecting for color, or just want to know more about the colorful blooms in your garden, you won’t want to miss Dr. Walbot’s presentation. There will be time for Q&A after the presentation.
