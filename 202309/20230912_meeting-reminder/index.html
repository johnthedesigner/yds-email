Are you ready for Sunday's garden tour?

Tufts Community Garden Tour and Tufts Pollinator Initiative Presentation

Sunday, September 15, 2023 from (10:00am*) 11:15am to 12:45pm

*Join us at Tu Y Yo Mexican Restaurant for an optional brunch beforehand

In the past we’ve had the pleasure of visiting lovely member gardens on a farm and in the suburbs. Now, we will enjoy a unique opportunity to visit a lovely urban garden. Tufts Community Garden is a large urban communal garden space located in the midst of a bustling university campus. YDS member Peter Heller has been gardening and growing dahlias there for the past 30 years and has held the role of Garden Coordinator since 2002. We will tour this lovely urban green space and get to meet other community gardeners, a number of whom also grow dahlias.

A garden is not complete without an abundance of insect pollinators. Karen Dooley, a master’s student and part of the Tufts Pollinator Initiative, led in a pollinator research project this summer at the Tufts Community Garden. Karen will touch on her research project and give us an overview of the Tufts Pollinator Initiative, what it strives to do, and why it matters. She will share with us more about the many insect pollinators around us and how to attract them to our gardens. Since now is the season for monarch butterfly migration activity we also will learn several fun facts and information about monarchs.

Join us as we tour and learn from Peter about the history and development of the Tufts Community Garden, and as we hear from Karen about the ongoing efforts of the Tufts Pollinator Initiative to educate about and conserve insect pollinators, and together enjoy this unique and lovely urban green space.
